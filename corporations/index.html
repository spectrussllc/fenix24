The Threat Landscape

No Industry Is Off the Threat List.

Modern threat actors do not discriminate. They target organizations with valuable data, reputations worth protecting, and incentives to pay quietly and move on. If that describes your organization, you are already on their radar.

Sensitive Data Equals High-Value Targets

Financial records, intellectual property, customer data, trade secrets — whatever your organization holds, threat actors have assigned it a dollar value and a strategy to extract it.

The Economics of Silence

Organizations pay ransoms quietly to avoid reputational fallout, regulatory scrutiny, and operational disruption. Cybercriminals know this. Your incentive to move on becomes their leverage.

Scattered Spider and Akira

These are not faceless risks. Groups like Akira represent Fenix24's number one breach pattern across all industries. They are organized, motivated, and methodical in how they operate.

Identity and Help Desk Exploits

The most common vulnerability is weak identity verification. Attackers social-engineer their way in through your own IT help desk before you even know an attack has begun.

Overcrowded Active Directories

Years of growth without cleanup leaves Active Directories cluttered with stale accounts and excessive permissions. Each one is an open door hiding in plain sight inside your own systems.

Operational and Reputational Fallout

A breach does not just cost money. It halts operations, triggers regulatory and legal exposure, and erodes the trust of customers, partners, and stakeholders you may never fully recover.
